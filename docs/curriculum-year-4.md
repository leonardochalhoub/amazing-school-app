# Year 4 — Post-C2 Professional Fluency

> **Status:** Planning document. Assumes completion of Years 1–3 (A1 → C2).
> **Scope:** Not a CEFR level. A post-proficiency specialization year for
> adults who have demonstrated C2 mastery and now want to deploy English
> in a specific professional domain and creative expression.

## Why Year 4

CEFR ends at C2. The Council of Europe's 2020 Companion Volume added
"above C2" descriptors for specialist and artistic registers but
deliberately refused to create a C3 — mastery is not linear from C2.
Year 4 is where a fluent speaker becomes a **native-like practitioner
of their craft**.

**Research and standards consulted:**

- Council of Europe. *CEFR Companion Volume* (2020) — descriptors for
  mediation, literature, telecollaboration, plurilingual competence.
- Cambridge English. *Proficiency exam (CPE)* specifications — the
  canonical C2 benchmark.
- IELTS Band 9 descriptors — "fully operational command; fully
  appropriate, accurate, fluent with complete understanding".
- TESOL International's *6 Principles for Exemplary Teaching* (2018).
- ACTFL Proficiency Guidelines — the "Distinguished" level beyond
  "Superior", focused on sustained discourse in specialized domains.

## Core thesis for Year 4

Year 4 is not more grammar. It is **deliberate practice in the
domains where the learner's English will actually be judged**:

1. **Professional conversation** — the user's specified primary focus.
   Meetings, negotiations, presentations, 1:1 mentoring, crisis calls.
   60% of the year.
2. **Sustained listening** — real unscripted content at native pace.
   20% of the year.
3. **Sustained reading** — long-form, unabridged, with interpretation.
   10% of the year.
4. **Sustained writing** — weekly essays, memos, contracts, scripts.
   10% of the year.

## Activity types per lesson (Year 4)

Every Y4 lesson contains at least four of the six:

1. **Dialog** — a 15–20 turn professional conversation. User records
   responses; Groq Whisper scores. (Already built.)
2. **Listening story** — a 10–15 paragraph unscripted-feel scene with
   5+ distinct character voices. Student writes a full interpretation
   (min 300 words) that the teacher reviews. (Built.)
3. **Text interpretation** — a literary or professional passage (500–
   1500 words). Student writes a response with a specific prompt.
4. **Speaking lab drill** — a 5+ sentence cold response recorded against
   a timed prompt (as in a real interview). (Built — extend to Y4.)
5. **Long-form reading** — one full chapter, essay, or investigation
   assigned between lessons (homework).
6. **Real-world exchange** — weekly 1:1 with a teacher (live class).

## Recommended external sources for Y4

### Reading (long-form)

- *The Economist* — business, policy, finance
- *The New Yorker* — long essays, fiction, reporting
- *Aeon / Psyche* — philosophy, psychology, long essays
- *London Review of Books* — literary criticism at the limit
- *The Atlantic* — US politics and culture
- *Harper's Magazine* — literary journalism and essays
- Project Gutenberg — full novels, free, public domain

### Listening (unscripted, native pace)

- *BBC In Our Time* — 45-min academic conversations with no script
- *The Ezra Klein Show* — long-form interviews on politics and ideas
- *Conversations with Tyler* — expert interviews, very dense
- *Radiolab* — science storytelling at native pace
- *Revisionist History (Malcolm Gladwell)* — essay-narrative form
- *The Daily* (NYT) — news plus context, 20-min daily

### Speaking practice

- **Toastmasters International** — structured public speaking clubs
  in almost every major city worldwide
- **iTalki / Preply** — 1:1 native speakers for paid conversation
- **Record-then-review** — weekly self-recordings with transcripts
- **Local English meetups** — unpaid conversation practice

### Writing

- *The Elements of Style* — Strunk & White (micro-craft)
- *On Writing Well* — William Zinsser (long-form)
- *Several Short Sentences About Writing* — Verlyn Klinkenborg
- *Bird by Bird* — Anne Lamott (creative writing discipline)
- **Daily journal** — 500 words per day in English, non-optional

### Tests and benchmarks for Y4

- **CPE (C2 Proficiency)** — Cambridge's highest certification
- **IELTS Academic** — aim for 8.5+
- **TOEFL iBT** — aim for 115+
- **ACTFL OPIc Distinguished** — oral proficiency at expert level

## Teacher's role in Year 4

At Y4 the teacher is less instructor, more **coach + editor + mirror**:

- Weekly 1:1 with timed recording + live feedback.
- Written work returned within 48h with line-level comments.
- Reading assignments discussed in depth, not summarized.
- Professional scenarios role-played with the teacher playing the
  adversarial party (hostile investor, skeptical journalist, angry
  board member).
- Explicit, compassionate, targeted critique — the student is no
  longer being protected from difficulty.

## Year 4 ends when

The student can, in a language that is not their first:

- Lead a one-hour professional meeting of strangers with ease.
- Read any major English-language newspaper or academic journal with
  full comprehension and native-like critical engagement.
- Write a 2,000-word essay publishable in *The Atlantic* or similar.
- Deliver a 20-minute speech with no notes and sustained coherence.
- Hold a 90-minute philosophical conversation with a native PhD.
- Interpret poetry closely enough to teach a single poem for an hour.

None of these are grammar goals. All are **practical fluency goals** —
the true end of language learning as a structured curriculum.

## Decisions taken (owner authorized)

**1. Seed batch, not full build.** 20 Y4 lessons are generated and
live (10 Y4.1 professional + 10 Y4.2 interpretive). The remaining
60 lessons from the full plan above will be authored incrementally
as pilot students provide feedback. Rationale: no students are at
Y4 yet, and blind-generating 80 more lessons risks being generic.

**2. Reuse existing scene kinds.** Y4 uses the same pipeline as
every other level — `listening_story` already handles
teacher-reviewed written interpretation, which covers the bulk of
Y4's interpretive and reflective work. No new scene kinds were
introduced. Future additions (`role_play`, `long_form_reading_log`)
can be added if pilot teachers find the existing scenes limiting.

**3. Async-first, with visible live-session recommendation.** Y4
lessons are reachable to any signed-in user who has completed C2.
The content itself can be done entirely asynchronously. A note
inside each Y4 lesson recommends pairing it with a weekly 1:1
teacher session — strongly encouraged, never gated.
