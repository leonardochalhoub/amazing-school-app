# Character Ecosystem — Amazing School US English Year 1

> A cast of recurring characters who appear across lessons to build continuity, pedagogical grounding, and emotional investment. Every lesson drawing on narrative content picks 1–3 characters from this roster.

All characters are fictional. Names chosen to feel authentic to a Brazilian learner without mapping to any specific real person.

---

## Core Cast

### 🎒 Maria Silva
- **Age:** 15
- **From:** São Paulo, Brazil → moved to Los Angeles, CA 18 months ago
- **Role:** Teenage student, curious, loves music (K-pop + MPB), learning guitar
- **Voice:** Eager, asks lots of questions, makes relatable beginner mistakes
- **Appears in:** A1.1–A2.2 (everywhere — our protagonist)
- **Visual cue:** Warm cartoon, dark wavy hair, hoop earrings, purple T-shirt (matches her in-app avatar)

### 🏀 Tom Walker
- **Age:** 16
- **From:** Los Angeles, CA (born and raised)
- **Role:** Maria's classmate and best friend at school, high school basketball player
- **Voice:** Casual US teen English, uses slang (respectfully), funny

## Narrative Principles

1. **Every reading uses named characters** — never "a boy", always "Tom" or "Maria".
2. **Spiral continuity** — if Maria learns to order coffee in A1.2, she orders a more complex drink in A2.2.
3. **Bilingual realism** — it's normal for Maria to code-switch; lessons show this respectfully.
4. **No stereotypes** — characters have inner lives, hobbies, quirks. Not props.
5. **Brazilian perspective** — cultural touchpoints (feijoada, futebol, Carnaval) appear naturally.
6. **Kindness** — nobody in the ecosystem is mean. This is a warm world where English is learned with joy.
